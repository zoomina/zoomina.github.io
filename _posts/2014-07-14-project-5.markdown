    ### KoBERT-Based Korean Multi-Class Emotion Classification + Hackathon Award
    #Python #NLP #SentimentAnalysis #KoBERT #PyTorch
    <br><br>
    <strong>Problem</strong>
    <ul style="list-style:none; padding-left:0;">
      <li>This project started with classifying emotions in Korean Twitter text and expanded into applying BERT-based classification to dialogue data in a hackathon setting.</li>
      <li>The goal was to test how well transformer models could capture nuanced emotional categories in Korean text.</li>
    </ul>
    <br>
    <strong>Data</strong>
    <ul>
      <li>Korean Twitter text labeled with joy, sadness, anger, and fear</li>
      <li>Conversation-style emotion classification data used in the MZ CEC hackathon</li>
    </ul>
    <br>
    <strong>Method</strong>
    <ul>
      <li>Fine-tuned KoBERT for multi-class emotion classification</li>
      <li>Compared performance by emotion category and analyzed weak classes</li>
      <li>Extended the approach to dialogue emotion classification in competition</li>
    </ul>
    <br>
    <strong>Insights</strong>
    <ul>
      <li>The Twitter model achieved about 95% accuracy for joy, sadness, and anger</li>
      <li>Fear showed relatively weaker performance, revealing class-level difficulty</li>
      <li>Based on this modeling experience, I placed 4th in the MZ CEC Emotion Dialogue Classification Hackathon</li>
    </ul>
